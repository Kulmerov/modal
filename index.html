Job hunting is the act of looking for employment, discontent with a current position, or a desire for a better opportunity. The immediate goal is usually to obtain an interview with an employer which may lead to getting hired.

Many job seekers research the companies to which they are applying, and some see evidence of this as a positive sign of enthusiasm for the position, or as a mark of thoroughness. Information collected might include a web-site, business description, year established, revenues, major products, competitors, and strengths and weaknesses.

Contacting as many people as possible is a highly effective way to find a job. It is estimated that 50% or higher of all jobs are found through networking. Job recruiters and decision makers are increasingly using online social networks to gather information about job applicants.
